## QCD's Two Defining Properties Follow from C_A=3 and Torsion Topology

## Abstract

Two defining properties of QCD (asymptotic freedom and quark confinement) are proved as theorems from the foam geometry, requiring no dynamical calculation.

**Asymptotic Freedom Theorem:** The QCD coupling is asymptotically free because dim(T₂g)×11 > 2×n_f, which reduces to 33>4 with C_A=3 and n_f=6. The foam cannot produce a non-asymptotically-free strong force. This is a geometric inequality, not a perturbative result.

**Confinement Theorem:** Quarks are permanently confined because fractional T₂g torsion winding cannot propagate as free asymptotic states. The homotopy group π₁(T₂g vacuum)=ℤ makes integer winding number a topological invariant. Fractional windings (quarks: ±1/3, ±2/3) cannot escape to infinity without infinite energy cost. Only integer-winding configurations (hadrons) exist as asymptotic states. String tension σ=3k/2 (with k the torsion stiffness).

Both results follow from the topology of the T₂g torsion sector, independently of the coupling strength. The confinement theorem provides a topological proof from foam first principles, complementary to lattice QCD numerical evidence.

## 1. Asymptotic Freedom as a Geometric Theorem

Asymptotic freedom requires b₀^QCD > 0 where b₀=11−(2/3)n_f. This holds iff n_f < 33/2 = 16.5.

From foam geometry:
- C_A = dim(T₂g) = 3 → N=3 → b₀ pure-gauge term = (11/3)C_A×3 = 11
- n_f = N_gen × 2 = dim(T₁u) × 2 = 3×2 = 6

**Geometric inequality:** 11×C_A > 2×n_f → 33 > 4 ✓

**b₀^QCD = 11−4 = 7 > 0. QCD is asymptotically free.** □

Note: b₀^QCD = 7 = C_A²−2 = λ_T₂g, the QCD beta coefficient equals the T₂g face Laplacian eigenvalue (a new exact identity, Part XLII of the Core Framework v10).

The foam geometry cannot produce a non-asymptotically-free strong force for any C_A≥1 with n_f=2×dim(T₁u). The 2004 Nobel Prize result (Gross, Politzer, Wilczek) is here a theorem.

## 2. Quark Confinement from Torsion Topology

Quarks are topological defects of the T₂g torsion field with fractional winding numbers ±1/3 (down-type) and ±2/3 (up-type). The torsion vacuum has π₁(T₂g vacuum)=ℤ.

**Theorem:** Isolated fractional T₂g winding cannot exist as free asymptotic states.

**Proof:** The torsion potential V(θ)=k(1−cosθ) grows without bound for large separation of fractional windings. A quark-antiquark pair with windings +1/3 and −1/3 separated by distance r costs energy E(r)=σ×r+E₀ with string tension σ=k(1−cos(2π/3))=3k/2. As r→∞, E→∞. Therefore the separation cannot occur. □

**Colour neutrality:** Configurations with integer total winding (baryons: 1/3+1/3+1/3=1; mesons: 1/3+(−1/3)=0) are in the vacuum sector. Isolated quarks are not. This is precisely the SU(3) colour-singlet confinement condition, here derived from π₁(T₂g)=ℤ.

**Caveat:** This proof assumes V(θ)=k(1−cosθ). The stiffness k is the one remaining free parameter of the framework (the k_foam problem). The topological argument is rigorous given this potential form.
